Modular, monocular-only navigation stack built for Team NUS's entry to the first Earth Rover Challenge (IROS 2024, Abu Dhabi): open-world, GPS-goal navigation on a low-cost robot, treating navigation as traversability estimation directly in image space rather than 3D reconstruction. The team placed 2nd among the three competing AI teams; the approach is documented in IEEE RAM's "Autonomous Mario Kart in the Wild" Competitions article.
